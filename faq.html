General

GENERAL QUESTIONS

Who can participate?
+
Grades 9–12, Duval/St. Johns/Nassau County schools. All sports welcome. Any high school athlete from any sport can compete at The 904 Combine.
When and where?
+
April 12, 2026 at Atlantic Coast High School, 9735 R.G. Skinner Pkwy, Jacksonville FL 32256.
How long does it take?
+
Plan for a full day: 8AM–3:30PM. Athletes can move at their own pace between stations. You don't need to compete in every station, so your schedule is flexible within the event window.
Do I need to play football?
+
No. Any high school athlete from any sport is welcome. Football, basketball, soccer, track, volleyball — if you're in grades 9–12 and want to get tested at NFL combine standards, you belong at The 904 Combine.

Registration & Pricing

REGISTRATION & PRICING

How much does it cost?
+
$60 athlete registration covers all 9 stations. The Parent Tackle Challenge is a $20 add-on. Your registration is 100% tax-deductible as a donation to Beary Good Sports LLC, a registered nonprofit.
Can I do just some stations?
+
Yes. Your $60 gives access to all stations, but you choose which ones to compete in. You can skip any station or leave early if needed.
Is there an age minimum?
+
Must be enrolled in grades 9–12. Parental consent required if under 18. We'll have a consent form available at registration for athletes under 18.
What is the refund policy?
+
Non-refundable (registered as donation). Athlete name transfer allowed up to 72 hours before event. Contact us directly to arrange a transfer.

Stations & Measurement

STATIONS & MEASUREMENT

How are results measured?
+
40-yard dash is laser-timed. Jumps use Vertec + tape measure. QB/Kicking use point scoring. Bench press uses rep count. All results are official and verified by trained staff using standardized NFL Scouting Combine methodology.
How many attempts do I get?
+
Speed events: 2 attempts. Jumps: 3 attempts. Scoring events (QB, kicking): 1 round. Strength (bench press): 1 set. Your best attempt is recorded on your official metrics card.
What if there's a tie?
+
Second verified attempt same day. If still tied, both athletes receive the same rank on the leaderboard.
Can I use my results for recruiting?
+
Yes. Your downloadable metrics card shows official results from an NFL-coached event. You can use your results in recruiting profiles, social posts, and coach communications with confidence.

Jacksonville Football Camp & Recruiting

JACKSONVILLE FOOTBALL QUESTIONS

Is there a Jacksonville Jaguars football camp for high school athletes?
+
Jaguars PREP focuses on youth/flag football. The 904 Combine features players and coaching. Not affiliated with the Jaguars franchise, but run by real Jaguars alumni.
How is The 904 Combine different from other Jacksonville football camps?
+
Verified NFL combine-style testing + Coaches + downloadable metrics card + 100% to. Most camps train. We measure. Our focus is on verified, official results, not just coaching.
Is there 7v7 at The 904 Combine?
+
We run a 5-on-5 competition station evaluating the same skills: QB reads, routes, coverage. Part of a full combine, not a standalone tournament. The 904 Combine is an all-day measurement event, not a 7v7 competition.
What schools can participate?
+
All Duval, St. Johns, Clay, and Nassau County high schools — including Atlantic Coast, Sandalwood, Mandarin, Raines, Bolles, Ponte Vedra, Nease, Yulee, Fernandina Beach, and more.
How does The 904 Combine compare to FBU or NFL FLAG?
+
FBU is a multi-day training program. NFL FLAG is a youth flag league. The 904 Combine is a one-day verified measurement event for high school varsity athletes, coached, benefiting a local.

Day-Of

WHAT TO EXPECT ON GAME DAY

What should I wear?
+
Athletic clothing and cleats. Bring a change of shoes for indoor stations. Wear what you'd wear to a practice or game — you want to perform in gear you're comfortable in.
What should I bring?
+
Water bottle, cleats and sneakers, any personal athletic equipment. We provide the rest — timing gates, measurement tools, scoring systems, and safety gear for the Parent Tackle Challenge.
Can parents attend?
+
Yes. Parents are welcome and encouraged to attend. Parent Tackle Challenge participants will definitely want their moms there.
How does the Parent Tackle Challenge work — is it safe?
+
Son stands completely stationary at the edge of a high jump pit. Mom runs up and drives the tackle — son falls safely backward into the padded pit. There is no head-on collision. Full safety gear is provided and required for mom. Event staff brief all participants on-site before the station.
What if it rains?
+
Light Rain: proceeds. Heavy/Lightning: 30-min delay. Severe: reschedule within 60 days. We'll stay in touch and announce any delays by 7:00 AM on event day.
